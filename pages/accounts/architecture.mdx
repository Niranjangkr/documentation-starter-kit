# Architecture

This page describes how Agile Receivables models customers, accounts, campaigns, payments, and disputes,
and how external systems integrate with the API.

## High-level system view

At a high level:

- Your **ERP / billing system** owns the source-of-truth invoices and customer master data.
- The **Agile Receivables API** accepts data for customers, accounts, campaigns, and payments.
- The **campaign engine** triggers communications and workflows based on account status.
- **Collectors and customers** interact with the system through portals and messaging channels.

## Data model relationships

Core entities relevant to the external API:

- **Customer** – person or business you invoice.
- **Account (invoice)** – a receivable linked to a customer, with amount, currency, issue date, and due date.
- **Campaign** – a defined series of steps that drive collections activity for a group of accounts.
- **Payment** – money applied to an account, reducing the outstanding balance.
- **Dispute** – a customer-raised issue related to an account, with a message thread and resolution lifecycle.

Typical relationships:

- A customer can have many accounts.
- An account can belong to at most one active campaign at a time.
- An account can have many payments and at most one active dispute.

## Validation and lifecycle flows

From an integration perspective, most flows follow this pattern:

1. Your system creates or updates customers.
2. Your system creates or updates accounts (invoices) for those customers.
3. Agile Receivables assigns accounts to campaigns and drives communications.
4. Customers make payments, which are recorded against accounts.
5. Status changes and disputes are synchronized back to your systems as needed.

## Multi-tenant and workspace model

Agile Receivables is multi-tenant:

- Each company has its own isolated tenant.
- Within a tenant, **workspaces** can be used to separate business units, brands, or regions.
- API keys can be scoped to a single workspace or to an organization-level view that spans multiple workspaces.
